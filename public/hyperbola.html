Hyperbola speed

The third cosmic speed is the minimum speed that must be given to a body located near the Earth's surface so that it can overcome the gravitational attraction of the Earth and the Sun and leave the solar system.

The escape velocity from Earth's surface is about 16.6 km/s.

When launched from the Earth, making the best use of the axial rotation (≈0.5 km / s) and the orbital motion of the planet (≈29.8 km / s), the spacecraft can reach the third space velocity already at ~ 16.6 km / s relative to the Earth. To exclude the influence of atmospheric drag, it is assumed that the spacecraft acquires this speed outside the Earth's atmosphere. The most energetically favorable start for reaching the third cosmic speed should be carried out near the equator, the movement of the object should be co-directed to the axial rotation of the Earth and the orbital motion of the Earth around the Sun. In this case, the speed of the apparatus relative to the Sun will be 29.8 + 16.6 + 0.5 = 46.9 km / s.

At the beginning of 2015, not a single spacecraft left the vicinity of the Earth at a third cosmic speed. The New Horizons spacecraft had the highest rate of leaving the Earth; this speed was 16.26 km / s (heliocentric speed 45 km / s), which is 0.34 km / s less than the third cosmic one. But due to the gravitational maneuver near Jupiter in 2007, he added 4 km / s, which will allow him to confidently leave the heliosphere in the future. At the end of the main part of their mission (exploration of Pluto), New Horizons were moving away from the Sun at a heliocentric speed of about 14 km / s. Other spacecraft that had already left the heliosphere (Voyager 1, Voyager 2, Pioneer 10 and Pioneer 11) accelerated in a similar way. All of them left the vicinity of the Earth at speeds significantly lower than the third cosmic one.
